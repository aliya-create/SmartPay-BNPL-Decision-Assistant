01 · The problem

The checkout sees one purchase. The customer sees everything.

A BNPL checkout typically presents payment options. But the customer is making a decision within a much larger payment calendar.

The checkout

SAR 3,499

A single purchase. A few monthly payment options. A decision that appears simple.

The customer

SAR 3,499 + everything else.

Existing commitments. Upcoming due dates. Multiple active plans. The customer's real decision is not just “Can I pay this?”

03 · How it works

Not “AI magic.” A decision system.

The product recommendation is designed around the customer's payment context, not just the purchase amount.

01

Purchase

What is the customer buying and how large is the new commitment?

02

Affordability

How would each plan change the customer's estimated monthly payment load?

03

Timing

Would the new payment create concentration around existing due dates?

04

Recommendation

Present the best-fit option with an explanation the customer can understand.

```
```
The product insight

The problem isn't payment. It's payment visibility.

The customer does not experience one installment plan in isolation. They experience the combined impact of every payment they have committed to.

```
```
04 · Product strategy

From visibility to prevention.

SmartPay could evolve from helping customers understand a decision to helping them anticipate the consequences of future commitments.

Phase 01

Explain

Show customers the monthly and timing impact of each available plan.

Phase 02

Recommend

Suggest a plan based on purchase amount, payment load, and upcoming commitments.

Phase 03

Anticipate

Identify when a new commitment could create payment concentration or stress.

Phase 04

Personalize

Build a more complete payment planning experience across the ecosystem.

```
```
The takeaway

Better checkout decisions start with better context.

SmartPay is not designed to decide for the customer. It is designed to make the consequences of each decision easier to understand.
